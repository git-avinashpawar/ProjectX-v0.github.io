Public Health Narratives Around Covid-19

Project Information

  • Category: Information visualization, Data Science
  • Project date: 25 April, 2022
  • Project URL: Github : Visualizing-Public-Narratives-Covid-19

Technologies used : Google BigQuery, Tableu, GDELT Project

View on Github

"Public Health Narratives Around Covid-19" is an immersive data-driven project developed at Indiana University, led by a team of four, with me as the project leader. Leveraging Google BigQuery on the GDELT dataset, which encompasses a vast 200 GB of data, the project delves into the exploration and analysis of Covid-19 trends.

COVID-19 garnered widespread media coverage, serving as a conduit for various narratives and viewpoints. The study necessitated collaboration with open-source tabular data sources, extensive data preparation, and the application of multiple visual analyses to cater to stakeholder needs.

The GDELT project played a pivotal role in providing comprehensive data for analysis. It involved information sourced from online and television news outlets, including the complete archives of BBC News London, CNN, MSNBC, and Fox News from January 1, 2020, to the present. Selected days surrounding past disease outbreaks were also incorporated from CNN, MSNBC, and Fox News, utilizing the Television News Archive.

The project's journey commenced with data exploration and understanding, followed by meticulous data preprocessing and the selection of suitable analyses and visualizations to address research questions. The team focused solely on the Covid-19 timeframe, restricting the data to the period after 2020.

Key aspects of the project included temporal analysis, geospatial analysis, topical analysis, and word clouds. These analytical approaches provided valuable insights into diverse patterns observed in different timeframes and specific circumstances, shedding light on the portrayal of Covid-19 in the media.

Moreover, the study identified intriguing changepoint timeslots that aligned with real-world events, further enhancing the project's findings. This collaborative effort at Indiana University not only contributed to a comprehensive understanding of the Covid-19 pandemic but also showcased the capabilities of the GDELT project as a valuable resource for data-driven analysis.

As a leader of the team, I spearheaded the project's execution, fostering collaboration and ensuring the successful completion of this impactful exploration into public health narratives surrounding Covid-19.
